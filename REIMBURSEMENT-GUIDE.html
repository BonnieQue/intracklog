User Guide

Reconcile & get reimbursed.

How to classify your trips, check that your mileage and spend line up, and claim back your business travel — and, for team admins, how to approve and pay those claims.

The idea in one minute

Not every kilometre you drive is for work. To claim travel back from an employer (or for SARS), you separate business driving from personal. InTrackLog now lets you do exactly that, then turns your business kilometres into a reimbursement claim you can send to your team for approval.

TermWhat it means
ClassifyTag each trip as Business or Personal. Only business trips can be claimed.
ReconcileA quick check that your trips and fuel/expenses agree (and that nothing's unclassified).
ReimbursableYour business kilometres × the per-km rate = the rand amount you can claim.
ClaimA reimbursement request you submit to a team for a given month.
TeamYour employer/workplace in the app. Admins of the team approve and pay claims.
Where to find it Everything lives under the Reports tile on your dashboard. Tap it, then switch between the Overview and Reconcile tabs at the top. Use the ‹ › arrows to pick the month.

For drivers  Classify & claim

Step A — Classify your trips

  1. Open Reports → Reconcile and choose the month with the ‹ › arrows.
  2. Find the Classify trips list — it shows every trip not yet tagged, with its date, distance and route.
  3. Tap Business or Personal on each one. It saves instantly and drops off the list.
  4. When the list is empty, every trip for that month is classified. ✅
Tip Do this little and often — a quick swipe through after a day's driving keeps month-end painless.

Step B — Check it reconciles

Still on the Reconcile tab, the cards and Things to check list give you a health check for the month:

You'll seeWhat it tells you
Business km · ReimbursableYour claimable distance and what it's worth in rand.
Fuel / km · All-in / kmYour running cost — handy for spotting an odd month.
Things to checkFlags like "fuel purchase with no trip within 3 days" or "trips but no fuel logged".

Step C — Submit your claim

  1. In the Submit for reimbursement section, each team you belong to shows your business km × rate = the amount.
  2. Tap Submit next to the right team.
  3. The button becomes a status badge so you always know where your claim stands:
    Submitted Approved Rejected Paid
Before you can submit You need to (1) classify at least one business trip that month, and (2) belong to a team. No team yet? Create one under Teams — you become its admin automatically.

For team admins  Approve & pay

If you own a team, or you're an admin member, you review what your drivers submit.

  1. Go to Dashboard → Teams and select your team.
  2. Open the Claims tab. A number next to it — e.g. Claims (2) — is how many are waiting on you.
  3. Each claim shows the driver, the month, the kilometres and the amount.
  4. Tap Approve or Reject. Approved claims then show a Mark paid button.
  5. Once you've paid the driver, tap Mark paid to close it off.
StatusMeaning
SubmittedWaiting for an admin to review.
ApprovedAccepted — now awaiting payment.
RejectedDeclined. The driver sees this and can fix & resubmit.
PaidReimbursed and closed.

How the amount is worked out

Reimbursable amount = business kilometres × the per-km rate. The rate comes from the team: if the team has a custom rate, that's used; otherwise the standard rate (R4.64/km) applies. The amount is locked in at the moment you submit, so an approved claim never changes later even if you log more trips.

Frequently asked

A trip isn't in my Classify list — why?

Only entries with a distance count as trips. Also check you're on the right month. Trips you've already classified won't reappear (they've done their job).

Can I change a classification later?

Yes — it only leaves the Classify list once tagged, but you can re-tag from your trip records at any time before you submit.

I'm a solo owner — can I test the whole flow myself?

Yes. Create a team (you're the admin), classify trips, submit a claim as yourself, then approve and mark it paid from the team's Claims tab.

Does this import my bank or fuel-card statement?

Not yet — that's a planned next step. For now, expenses are the ones you've entered in the app, and the Reconcile checks compare them against your logged trips.
